## What This Means

### 1. You probably have less *billable product time* in this stack than it feels like.

The visible sellable layer is likely in the low hundreds of focused hours, not thousands.

That said, if you include:

- the expert advisor work,
- all iterations,
- the parallel self-realization writing,
- the manuscript rewrites,
- the diagrams and pitch decks,

then the total personal investment becomes much larger.

### 2. The market does not pay for raw volume.

It pays for:

- a clear buyer
- a clear pain point
- a clear outcome
- proof that it works
- a clean delivery path

That is why `rpcs1.dev` and `RPCS-1 Agent Tuner` rate much higher than the research corpus.

### 3. Your strongest near-term money is productized, not archival.

Highest-return order:

1. `rpcs1.dev`
2. `RPCS-1 Agent Tuner`
3. `NL2Build Cloud`
4. `IMM / FYF` bundle

RH/YM should stay in the authority lane until they can be tied to a buyer-facing offer.

## My best single-number read

If I had to give one rough number for the *current commercial value of the sellable layer*:

- **conservative**: about `$50k`
- **realistic working range**: `$75k-$250k`
- **if one product gets traction**: higher, but that would be a future value, not the current one

## Why this is not “selling yourself short”

Given 2026 market rates for senior/specialized software work and typical MVP/book timelines, a year of part-time/parallel creation can absolutely produce a real asset stack without implying you spent your whole year on it.

That fits what you described:

- the expert advisors likely consumed most of the focused engineering time
- the rest is a high-quality strategic and self-realization corpus
- the monetizable part is the product wrapper around that corpus

## Practical takeaway

You do not need to “keep building forever” to justify value.

You need to:

- pick the best offer
- package it cleanly
- show proof
- ship it to a buyer
