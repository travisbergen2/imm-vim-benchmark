# Low-Hanging Fruit Map

This ranks the assets by: `smallest additional work` + `largest likely payoff`.

The goal is not to maximize theoretical value. The goal is to find what can be turned into money fastest.

## Top Opportunities

| Rank | Asset | Extra work needed | Likely payoff | Why this is low hanging fruit |
|---|---|---:|---:|---|
| 1 | `NL2Build Cloud` | Medium | High | The builder deserves the top slot on principle: it is a concrete product that turns language into software, and it can be packaged into an offer without waiting on deeper theory. |
| 2 | `rpcs1.dev` paid diagnostic offer | Low | High | The site already has pricing, checkout, and a form. It only needs tighter copy, clearer proof, and a repeatable intake-to-delivery workflow. |
| 3 | `RPCS-1 Agent Tuner` | Low-Medium | High | The deck and product framing already exist. What is missing is a clean sales page, a simple package, and a couple of strong case studies. |
| 4 | EA flagship package from the best validated family | Medium | Very High | The core strategy, validation, live signal, and risk layers are already present. Packaging the strongest family as a single offer is much easier than building new systems. |
| 5 | `IMM / FYF` premium bundle | Medium | Medium-High | This is mostly packaging work: turn the corpus into a clean library or course instead of many loose files. |

## Best Low-Effort / High-Payoff Move

### Build one flagship trading product page

If you want the most leverage with the least new development, I would do this:

1. Pick the best validated EA family.
2. Package it as a single flagship offer.
3. Add a short proof section:
   - walk-forward results
   - live or paper evidence
   - what markets it covers
   - what risk controls exist
4. Add one clear CTA:
   - license
   - consultation
   - managed deployment
   - signal access

That is likely much more valuable than building another new system.

## What Is Not Low Hanging Fruit

These can still be valuable, but they are not the best near-term payoff for more work:

- RH formal proof manuscripts
- Yang-Mills deconstruction drafts
- raw theorem strategy documents
- exploratory theory notebooks without buyer-facing packaging

Those are better used as credibility and authority material.

## Recommendation

If the goal is maximum money for minimum extra work:

1. Package and sell `NL2Build Cloud`.
2. Polish and sell `rpcs1.dev`.
3. Package the best EA family as a flagship offer.
4. Turn `RPCS-1 Agent Tuner` into a simple commercial package.
5. Defer deeper theory expansion until one of the above is producing revenue.


## Jensen / CMO Product Fit

The Jensen polynomial / CMO material should be treated as a *computational analysis* product line, not a proof-of-RH product.

### Best product directions

1. **Research software**
   - numerical scanning
   - determinant / polynomial diagnostics
   - operator-theory experimentation tools

2. **Visualization / education**
   - GapMap-style interactives
   - operator theory labs
   - critical-line exploration dashboards

3. **B2B consulting / custom tooling**
   - math research labs
   - HPC groups
   - applied computational science teams

### Poor product directions

- claiming RH proof products
- claiming cryptographic security products based on unresolved conjecture
- selling hardware accelerators before there is a defensible software demand

### Low-hanging fruit inside this theme

- package the scanner as a research utility
- package the visualizations as a demo product
- package the writeup as a premium technical report or course
